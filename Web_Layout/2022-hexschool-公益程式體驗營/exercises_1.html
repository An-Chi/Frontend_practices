Taiwan posts 3.06% GDP growth in first quarter (2022/04/28)

Taiwan's economy performed better than expected in the first quarter, expanding by 3.06%. That's according to preliminary data released Thursday by the government's statistics agency. A previous forecast had put GDP growth at 3% for the first quarter. The agency said that exports had performed better than expected, rising 23.5% compared to the previous year. It expects annual GDP growth to reach 4.43%.

However, consumer spending fell short of expectations in the first quarter, taking a hit from rising inflation. Consumption rose slightly by 0.23%, falling short of a projection from February. Officials said that in the second quarter, economic growth will be affected if the epidemic worsens and if China's COVID lockdowns persist.
